Our Mission

We are dedicated to preserving and protecting the rich biodiversity of Nigeria's wildlife, safeguarding the rich biodiversity of Nigeria by protecting and conserving its wildlife and natural habitats. Through strategic initiatives, community engagement, and innovative conservation practices, we aim to ensure the sustainable coexistence of humans and wildlife, preserving Nigeria's natural heritage for future generations. We are committed to combating illegal poaching, habitat destruction, and wildlife trafficking while promoting environmental education and fostering partnerships with government agencies, local communities, and international organizations. Together, we strive to create a future where wildlife thrives in harmony with Nigeria's diverse ecosystems, enriching the lives of all who call this land home.

Our Achievements

Species Recovery and Reintroduction Programs:

Many wildlife conservation initiatives have successfully rescued species from the brink of extinction through captive breeding and reintroduction programs. One exemplary case is the California condor, which faced imminent extinction in the 1980s due to habitat destruction and lead poisoning. Through intensive conservation efforts, including captive breeding and habitat restoration, the California condor population has rebounded from a mere 22 individuals to over 400 birds in the wild today. Similarly, species such as the Arabian oryx, the black-footed ferret, and the European bison have been saved from extinction through coordinated conservation efforts involving captive breeding and reintroduction into suitable habitats

Protected Area Expansion and Management:

Governments and conservation organizations have worked tirelessly to establish and manage protected areas, such as national parks, wildlife reserves, and marine sanctuaries. These areas serve as havens for wildlife by providing secure habitats, safeguarding critical ecosystems, and mitigating human-wildlife conflicts. Notable examples include the Yellowstone National Park in the United States, Serengeti National Park in Tanzania, and the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park in Australia. These protected areas not only conserve biodiversity but also support ecotourism, research, and education, contributing to local economies and raising awareness about the importance of wildlife conservation.

Community-Based Conservation Initiatives:

Recognizing the importance of engaging local communities in conservation efforts, many organizations have implemented community-based conservation programs. These initiatives empower communities to take ownership of their natural resources, promote sustainable livelihoods, and reduce human-wildlife conflicts. For instance, projects like the CAMPFIRE (Communal Areas Management Programme for Indigenous Resources) in Zimbabwe and the Namibian Community-Based Natural Resource Management Program have demonstrated how involving local communities in conservation decision-making can lead to more effective and sustainable outcomes. By providing economic incentives, such as revenue-sharing schemes from ecotourism and sustainable hunting, these programs foster positive attitudes towards wildlife conservation among local populations while simultaneously protecting biodiversity and habitats.
