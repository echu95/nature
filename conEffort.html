Our Conservation Initiatives

The Plastic Pollution Reduction campaign is a multifaceted initiative that aims to combat the widespread environmental degradation caused by plastic pollution, utilizing strategies such as public education, policy advocacy, community engagement, technological innovation, and corporate responsibility partnerships. Through its comprehensive approach, the campaign seeks to raise awareness about the detrimental impacts of plastic waste, advocate for regulatory measures to reduce plastic usage, empower local communities to participate in cleanup efforts, promote the development of sustainable alternatives to plastic, and foster collaborative efforts among various stakeholders to address this urgent global issue.

The rewilding movement represents a paradigm shift in conservation, advocating for the restoration of natural ecosystems through strategies like habitat restoration, species reintroduction, community engagement, scientific research, and education, fostering healthier landscapes that support biodiversity and ecosystem resilience.

Our Projects

The Great Green Wall (Africa):

The Great Green Wall is a massive project across Africa aimed at combatting desertification and land degradation. It involves planting a wall of trees stretching over 8,000 kilometers across the continent, from Senegal in the west to Djibouti in the east. This initiative aims to restore degraded land, improve food security, and create sustainable livelihoods for millions of people living in the region.

The Coral Triangle Initiative (Southeast Asia):

The Coral Triangle Initiative is a collaborative effort among six countries in Southeast Asia (Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines, Papua New Guinea, Timor-Leste, and the Solomon Islands) to protect the marine biodiversity of the Coral Triangle. This region is home to the highest diversity of coral reef species in the world. The initiative focuses on marine conservation, sustainable fisheries management, and community engagement to safeguard the rich marine resources and support the livelihoods of coastal communities.

The Tiger Conservation Projects (Various countries):

Tiger conservation projects are implemented in several countries where tigers are found, such as India, Nepal, Bangladesh, Russia, and others. These projects aim to protect and restore tiger habitats, combat poaching and illegal wildlife trade, and promote human-tiger coexistence. One notable example is India's Project Tiger, launched in 1973, which has led to the establishment and management of tiger reserves across the country, contributing to the recovery of tiger populations.
